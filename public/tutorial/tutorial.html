Welcome to Picklebrawl!

Picklebrawl is a team management sports simulator game that plays right in your browser! Learn the basics of how to play here.

Getting Started

To begin, create an account on the login page. Then, create or join a league! (You may need to reload the page to see it show up on your homepage.) You can share the League Name and Password with your friends so you can play against them.

Friendlies

Friendlies are games that are played before the tournament starts. (Tournaments haven't been implemented yet, but you can play as many friendlies as you'd like.) You can play with your friends to get a sense of strategy for your own team, and learn about theirs.

View the league you've joined by clicking the View button on the homepage. Along the right-hand side, you will see all of the teams in the league, including yours!

To challenge another team, click the "Challenge" button by their team name. They will have to accept your challenge (done on the same page, by clicking the "Accept Challenge" button). Once accepted, both of you can "View Challenge".

Preparing for a Game

Picklebrawl is all about team management. Choose players that work well together and put them on roles that they will excel in. Once your team is set, you have no control over how the match goes. The match will play on its own once both teams have locked in their players and strategy, and you don't even have to watch it happening live -- you can watch it after its over, whenever you have time to log on!

  • Click on the "View Challenge" button next to a team where both of you have agreed to a challenge.
  • First, you will choose your players from your bench. You will not be able to see which players your opponent has chosen at this point, although you can see their entire team and guess what choices they may make. Once you're confident with your choices, press "Lock In Starters"!
  • Until both you and your opponent have locked in your starters, the person who locked in starters first will only see a greyed out bench with the option to undo their lock-in. Once both players lock in, there's no going back.
  • There are currently some bugs with these starters showing up, so you may need to use the "Back" button on the upper-left hand corner of the page, then press "View Challenge" again until the menus show as expected.
  • Second, once both challengers have locked in their starters, you will choose each starter's role. Each player has one role for when your team is on offense and one role for when your team is on defense. More detail on these is given in the "Roles" section of the tutorial. At this stage, you will be able to see the starters your opponent has picked. Once you're done picking roles, press "Lock In Actions"!
  • Again, until both players have locked in their actions, the player who locked in first will only see a greyed out menu with the option to undo their lock in. If your opponent is already locked in, you will see "READY" in green under their name on the action-picking menu. Once both players lock in their actions, the final stage of the challenge happens!

Playing a Game

Once both players have locked in their players and actions, the game will play itself. If you were on the action-picking menu when the game starts, it will automatically show you the game -- otherwise you'll have to press "View Challenge" to watch the animation.

The animation will show you a tick-by-tick play of how the game went, with visuals showing which team has possession, where it is on the field, and which players currently have the ball. There is a running console log as well which helps you keep track of which players did what during the game.

Player Info/Stats

Each time you join a league, a team of 8 players is automatically generated for you. Each of these players has unique names, stats, and quirks. When planning your team, you can always mouse over any stat or quirk to see a brief description. There are currently 6 stats a player can have, each of which giving the player different strengths:

  • Bulk: Players with high values take less damage when attacked and are better at defending advancement from the other team
  • Finesse: Players with high values are better at scoring and assisting other players
  • Height: Players with high values are better at defending scoring and protecting their teammates from attacks
  • Strength: Players with high values are better at advancing and attacking
  • Trickiness: This stat is tied to Focus. If this player is on offense (advancing or scoring) and they're being defended by a player with lower Focus than this player's Trickiness, then this player has a chance to juke out the defender and advance/score as if they were not defended at all.
  • Focus: This stat is tied to Trickiness. If this player is on defense (defending advancing or scoring) and they're defending against a player with lower Trickiness than this player's Focus, then this player has no chance of getting juked out and always defends, lessening the chance of a large advance/successful score.

Each player also generates with a quirk. Learn about them as you play!

Roles

There are some roles players can only have while they're playing offense, some players can only have while they're playing defense, and some that are universal which can be used regardless of whether the player is playing offense or defense. Multiple players can have the same roles. When choosing roles for your starters, if you hover the mouse over a role that has been selected for a player, a tooltip will appear to describe what that role does.

Offensive Roles

  • Advance: This player will attempt to move towards the opponent's goal once your team is in possession of the ball. This role is aided by high Strength.
  • Score: This player will attempt to score once your team has advanced close enough to the opponent's goal. This role is aided by high Finesse.

Defensive Roles

  • Defend Advance: This player will lessen the amount that opposing players are able to move towards your goal when your team doesn't have possession of the ball. This role is aided by high Bulk.
  • Defend Score: This player will lessen the chances of an opposing Scorer making a successful goal when they try to shoot. This role is aided by high Height.

Universal Roles

  • Attack: This player will attack a player on the opposing team. If successful, the player who was attacked will have significantly lower stats depending on how much damage was done for a brief time. This role is aided by high Strength, and players with high Bulk naturally take less damage from attacks. Note that this feature is still being balanced!
  • Protect: This player will protect another player on your team from being attacked. If you have a star Scorer with very low Bulk, for example, giving them a Protector might prevent the opposing team from attacking that Scorer for huge amounts of damage and ruining your whole strategy. This role is aided by high Height.
  • Assist: This player will help another player on your team, whatever their role is. This role is aided by Finesse. This comes particularly in handy if you want two players on the same role, but only one player with good stats for that role, e.g. you want two Advancers but only have one player with high Strength. In this case, if you have a player with high Finesse, you can have them assist the Advancer, and it will be almost as good as simply having two advancers. Furthermore, if you have no idea what starters your opponent might pick, choosing a starter with high Finesse allows you more flexibility when it comes to possible strategies you might pick during your choosing roles stage.
  • Rest: This player will do nothing during the section of the game this role is picked for (offense or defense), but will become more effective at their role during the other section of the game.
